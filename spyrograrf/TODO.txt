1. Pinch / Bulge (your idea)

Imagine a distortion centered somewhere on the canvas.

As the pen approaches that point:

Pinch → points get pulled inward

Bulge → points get pushed outward

Visually this would make:

flowers collapse toward the centre

torus shapes cave in

spirals warp into vortexes

And because the line is drawn sequentially, you'd get these elastic-looking bends rather than a uniform distortion.

Even cooler:
Move the center off-centre and the whole drawing starts to look gravitational.

2. Gravity wells

Same concept but with strong falloff.

The curve behaves like it's passing near a planet.

Near the well:

lines bend

loops stretch

density increases

You could even allow multiple wells.

That would create beautiful tangled filaments.

3. Flow fields

Instead of a radial distortion, the point gets nudged by a vector field.

Think:

wind

fluid flow

magnetic lines

Your nice geometric curve slowly turns into something organic.

Spirographs suddenly look like:

smoke

coral

alien plants

Your particle system background means this idea is probably very natural for you.

4. Rotational vortex

Points near a centre get rotated slightly before drawing.

So instead of pulling inward or outward, they swirl.

Effects:

flowers twist

torus shapes become spirals

dense regions form whirlpools

You’d get galaxy-looking results very easily.

5. Wave distortions

Add a sine displacement.

Example conceptually:

horizontal ripple

vertical ripple

radial ripple

This would produce woven patterns.

Your dense torus shapes would become something like fabric or moiré interference.

6. Mirror fields

Points reflect across invisible lines.

Examples:

vertical mirror line

diagonal mirror

radial mirrors

This creates instant symmetry engines.

Your single curve suddenly becomes mandala generators.

7. Distance-based scaling

Instead of moving the point, scale it relative to centre.

So far points expand, near points compress.

This can create:

tunnel / wormhole effects

strong depth illusions

Which you already glimpsed with your “wormhole” shape.

8. Time-varying distortion (this one is wild)

What if the distortion itself changes slowly as the curve draws?

Examples:

pinch strength oscillates

vortex rotates slowly

gravity wells drift

Then the curve is reacting to a moving environment.

You’d get patterns that look alive.

9. Density distortions

Instead of moving points, distort time.

Meaning:

slow the curve near certain regions

speed it up elsewhere

You’d get dense clusters and airy regions without touching the geometry.

This is surprisingly powerful visually.

10. The idea is depth projection.

Not fake depth by shading — actual geometric depth simulated while drawing.

Your curve still exists in 2D math, but before drawing you project it through a fake 3D camera.
